## THE BASICS

- **Teams:** 3–5 members
- **Who can play:** 1st, 2nd, and 3rd year students
- **Device:** Each member needs a phone or laptop with internet
- **Join at:** `http://[EVENT_URL]` — enter your team passcode

Your team gets a unique **4-digit passcode** — this is your identity throughout the event. Keep it private.

---

## PHASE 1 — THE GAUNTLET
### 🧠 Individual Speed + Logic | ~50 minutes

This is the filter. Everyone starts. Most will fall.

### How It Works
1. A question appears on your screen simultaneously for all teams
2. You have **20–45 seconds** to submit your answer
3. The faster you answer correctly, the more points you earn
4. At the end of each round, the admin eliminates the lowest-scoring teams
5. 5 rounds total — survive all 5 to advance

### Scoring
```
Your Score = Base Points + (Time Limit − Time You Took)
```
Example: 20-point question, 40s limit, answered in 10s → 20 + 30 = **50 points**

### Question Types
| Round | Type | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1–2 | Multiple Choice | "Which data structure uses LIFO?" |
| 3–4 | Multiple Choice | "What is the time complexity of binary search?" |
| 5 | Text Input | "What does [1,2,3].reduce((a,b)=>a+b, 0) return?" |

### Rules
- No googling. No asking friends outside your team.
- First submission counts — no re-attempts per round.
- If you don't submit before timer → 0 points for that round.
- Admin decides how many teams to cut per round.

## PHASE 2 — CAMPUS CONQUEST
### 🗺️ Territory Battle | 90 minutes

Your campus is the battlefield. Your battery is your life.

### Setup
- The campus has **6 Strategic Zones** (each with one of our team members stationed there)
- Your team starts with **100% Battery Life**
- Battery drains **2% per minute** — constantly

### The Goal
Capture the most zones before 90 minutes is up. Top 4–5 teams advance to Phase 3.

### How to Capture a Zone
1. **Physically walk** to a campus zone as a team
2. Enter your **team passcode** on the laptop at that zone
3. **Important:** This costs you **5% battery** (non-refundable)
4. A coding/logic challenge appears
5. **You and your team solve it together** — then type your answer directly on the laptop
6. **Correct?** → Zone captured! Your battery goes up **+20%**
7. **Wrong?** → Zone stays neutral. Your 5% is gone. Try again or go elsewhere.

### Defending Your Zone
Once you capture a zone, **you must set a trap** for other teams:
- Go to your team dashboard
- Select "Set Defense Problem" for that zone
- Pick 1 challenge from 5 options — this becomes what attackers must solve to steal from you
- Choose wisely! Make it hard but fair.

### Stealing an Enemy Zone
1. Go to an enemy zone (one that another team owns)
2. Enter your passcode (costs 5% battery)
3. The defending team's challenge appears
4. Solve it → zone is yours, +20% battery
5. Wrong → zone stays with the defender

### Battery Rules
```
Start: 100%
Every minute: -2%
Attempt a zone: -5%
Capture a zone: +20%
Hit 0%: ELIMINATED
```

Warnings are sent at 20% and 10% — act fast.

### Important Zone Rules
- **Only one team at a time** can attempt a zone — if someone is already there, wait
- The zone laptop will show "ZONE BUSY" — you'll see it
- Zone operators are **neutral** — they cannot help you solve the challenge
- If you have a dispute, raise your hand and call a coordinator

## PHASE 3 — FINAL BUILD
### 💻 Build Under Pressure | 2.5 hours

Only the top teams make it here. Now prove you can build.

### What Happens
1. The admin reveals a **problem statement** — your mission
2. Your team has **2.5 hours** to build a working web application
3. Submit your project via the team dashboard before time runs out

### What to Submit
- **Deployment URL** — a live link (Vercel, Netlify, Railway, etc.)
- **GitHub URL** — your code repository
- **Notes** — features, tech stack, and any challenges faced

### Judging Criteria
- **Functionality** (Does it work?)
- **Creativity** (Is it innovative?)
- **Code Quality** (Is it clean and well-structured?)
- **Presentation** (Can you explain it clearly?)
